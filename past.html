INFOSCI 102 Computation and Problem Solving

An introduction to the fundamentals of computational thinking, such as decomposition, data representation, pattern recognition, and algorithms. This course focuses on the basic concepts and ideas behind computation. The course also offers a hands-on programming practice on producing clear, robust, and reasonably efficient code using top-down design, informal analysis, and effective testing and debugging. Starting from first principles, we will cover a large subset of the Python programming language, including its standard libraries and programming paradigms.

INFOSCI 103 Computation, Culture and Society

Computation, society, and culture are tightly interwoven into each other, and are unthinkable without each other. They each provide the means and frameworks in which the others develop. In this course, we’ll investigate a wide array of computational technologies and delve into the dynamic interactions these technologies have with society and culture, emphasizing the themes of innovation and maturation, ethics, collaboration and communication, and risk and failure.

CULANTH 201 New Media and Society

New media – ranging from Yahoo! to Facebook to WeChat and TikTok – have in recent years drastically influenced social, economic, and political life throughout the world. This course takes a social scientific approach to understanding the dynamics of new media and society, with particular emphasis on everyday life and social interactions. This course also adopts a global perspective, including research conducted both in the Global North and South, to trace the development of new media, their relationship to previous modes of communication and transmission, as well as how digital technologies influence emerging and changing spheres of economic, political and social exchanges. By the end of the class, students will be able to articulate the role of new media in society and the debates around emerging technologies, and different ways that can lead to actions for social change.

INFOSCI 201 Interaction and Physical Computing

Interaction and Physical Computing is a practice-based course designed to teach basic programming and electronics skills in the context of critical media and creative technology. The course is made up of two parallel focuses: 1) introducing programming and electronics through the Processing and Arduino IDE and hands-on exercises; 2) introducing essential concepts in interaction design through project-driven learning.
